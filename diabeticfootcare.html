    Diabetic Foot Care

    Diabetes is a chronic condition characterized by high blood Sugar levels that can have a significant impact on various parts of the body, including the feet. Diabetic foot care is a vital aspect of managing diabetes and preventing potentially severe complications. By adopting a comprehensive foot care routine, individuals with diabetes can maintain optimal foot health, reduce the risk of ulcers and infections, and enhance overall well-being.

    Book an Appointment

    Daily foot care practices:-

    1. Inspecting the feet:
      Individual with diabetes should inspect their feet daily for any signs of blisters, cuts redness, swelling Or calluses. By conducting a thorough examination, potential problems Can be identified early, allowing for timely intervention and prevention of complication
    2. Washing and drying:
      Feet should be washed gently with warm and mild soop. It is crucial to avoid soaking the feet for an extended period as it can lead to maceration. After washing, the feet should be carefully dried, especially between the toes, to prevent the growth of bacteria and fungal infections.
    3. Moisturizing:
      Applying a diabetic- friendly moisturizer to the feet, except between the toes, helps maintain skin hydration and prevent dryness and cracking. However, it is essential avoid to applying moisturizer between the toes as excessive moisturizer in this area can contribute to fungal infections.
    4. Nail Care:-
      Trimming to nails regularly, Straight across can prevent ingrown toenails and reduce the risk of infections. It is advisable to seek professional assistance if individuals have difficulty reaching (or) seeing their feet, (or) if they have thickened or fungal inflected nails.
    5. Protection:-
      Wearing clean, dry socks made of breathable materials, such a cotton (or) moisture-wicking fabrics, helps prevent excessive moisture buildup and reduces the risk of fungal infections. If is recommended to avoid tight - fitting (or) restrictive foot wear that can cause friction and pressure points.

    Foot wear selection:-

    Proper footwear selection is a critical aspect of diabetic foot care. I11-fitting shoes can lead to foot ulcers, calluses, and even foot deformities. By following these guidelines, individuals can make informed choices and maintain healthy feet.

    1. Shoe size and fit:-
      Shoes should be properly fitted, Providing ample space for the toes to move freely. It is advisable to have both feet measured, as foot size can change over time. Shoes should not be too tight or too loose, and there should be sufficient cushioning and support.
    2. Protective toe box :-
      Shoes with aroomy and protective toe box can prevent pressure points, blisters, and calluses. The toe box should have sufficient depth to accommodate any deformities (or) orthotic devices that individuals may require.
    3. Avoiding high heels and pointed shoes :-
      High heels and pointed shoes can place excessive pressure on certain ares of the foot and increase the risk of foot problems. It is best to opt for shoes with a low heel and a wide base for better stability and weight distribution

    Regular check-ups and early intervention :-

    • In addition to daily foot care practices and appropriate footwear, individuals with diabetes should also prioritize regular check-ups with their health care providers.
    • Regular foot examinations by a health care professional, such as a podiatrist, can help detect any underlying issues and ensure early intervention.
    • During these check-ups, the health care provider will assess the over all foot health, check for any signs of neuropathy(nerve damage), Peripheral vascular disease, (or) other diabetic foot complications. They may also perform tests to evaluate the sensation & circulation in the feet.
